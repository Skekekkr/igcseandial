Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about our courses and medical school preparation
What are the differences between the two medical schools in Hong Kong?

Both HKU and CUHK provide top-tier medical education. The key differences are:

  • HKU encourages more self-learning through problem-based learning (PBL) and senior support.
  • CUHK provides more structured guidance throughout learning.
  • HKU's medical faculty is located further from the main campus, making it harder to participate in general university activities.
  • CUHK offers better integration with non-medical peers.
What is IAL?

International A-Level (IAL) is a globally recognized qualification for students aiming to attend universities worldwide.

  • In Hong Kong, non-DSE students enter university through the non-JUPAS route.
  • Most university programs require three A-Level subjects, while medicine in Hong Kong requires four.
  • Some programs, such as mathematics and engineering, have specific subject requirements and admission tests.
